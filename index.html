8TH GLOBAL RESEARCH NETWORK – BELT AND ROAD INITIATIVE (GRN-BRI) CONFERENCE

The Greening, Reliability and Security of Global Maritime Transportation and Logistics
Ningbo University, Zhejiang, China
November 10-12, 2023

You can access the offical website of GRN-BRI 2023 to obtain more information !!!

Since the inception of the Belt and Road Initiative (BRI) in 2013, 10 years have been passed. Meanwhile, China-US trade friction, breakout of COVID-19, conflict between Russia and Ukraine have been envisaged. In the complex and ever-changing international political and economic landscape, global industrial and supply chains are changing, while as a critical element of the global supply chain, maritime transportation and logistics are facing a series of new opportunities and challenges. Moreover, the China-Railway Express, which has played an important role in international trade transportation since the BRI in 2013, will also face some new situations in the Eurasian continent. At this moment, we welcome the 8th annual conference of The Global Research Network Belt - Road Initiative (GRN-BRI). The GRN-BRI having members from 60 institutes in 40 countries along the Belt and Road is an independent research network providing a global platform for researchers to share and collaborate on research projects in the field of logistics, transport, international trade, and supply chain (https://grn-bri.com/). This yearly conference is one of the serial international conferences, industry seminars, and training workshops that disseminate the latest research and strategic insights to enhance policy-making and support economic development.

As the organizers of this conference, we are very happy that finally it is able for scholars from various concerning fields to gather onsite and have face-to-face discussions on various topics related to international trade transportation and shipping logistics in this conference, which aims to explore and discover the trends in international trade, transportation, and shipping logistics under the new world environment and situation. It also aims to analyze the challenges faced by stable port and international transportation and logistics supply chains.

Here, on behalf of the organizer of this annual conference, we sincerely invite all academic professionals, government policy-makers, and practitioners, to come to the beautiful and port city of Ningbo, which is the world’s largest port, to engage in academic exchanges at Ningbo University. This event will provide the participants with exchanges of views and insights into the greening, reliability, and security of global maritime transportation and logistics and supply chain. The theme of this year’s conference is “The Greening, Reliability and Security of Global Maritime Transportation and Logistics”. Topics of particular interest include, but are not limited to:

  • Events’ impacts on economic globalization and global industrial chain and the supply chain
  • Economic implications of BRI Regionalism versus Multilateralism
  • The future position of container hub ports in Asia
  • The impact of regional economy and culture on the logistics and supply chains in the BRI
  • Integration of Asia-Euro shipping liner and China Railway Express Liner
  • Multimodal international trade transportation coordination
  • Comparison of Reliabilities of shipping liners between Pacific and Indian oceans
  • The role of information technology in shipping and port logistics
  • The complex network of maritime terminals, inland ports, cross-border corridors
  • The network of free trade zones in the BRI
  • Sustainability issues along the Belt and Road Country
  • Maritime transportation and logistics competition and efficiency
  • Digitalization of shipping transportation: policy and practice
  • Global sourcing and production networks
  • Data analytics in global logistics and supply chain networks
  • Social impact of shipping logistics and political economy related to BRI
  • Maritime security international relations
  • Big data and data mining in logistics and supply chain management
  • Internet of Things (IoT) in logistics and supply chain management
  • Infrastructure for supply chain/ transportation/energy
  • Impacts of the COVID-19 on maritime transportation and logistics
  • Decarbonization of maritime industry
  • Green shipping corridors in tandem with decarbonization and digitalization
  • Greening the Belt and Road
  • Green investment for supply chain
  • Challenges and opportunities in Cruise shipping in the post-COVID-19 era
